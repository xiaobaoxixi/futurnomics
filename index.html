Women & Entrepreneurship

It has been shown that female entrepreneurship is a powerful economic driver as well as global trend.

Governments, institutions, politicians, universities all have female entrepreneurship on their agenda. And more than ever, women are ready, are experiencing, in a sense, the WOMENTUM.

On the other hand, data shows that female entrepreneurs are still facing many barriers to access financing to grow their enterprises worldwide.

Impact

Between 6.3 and 10.5 million additional jobs can be created in 2050 due to improvements in gender equality by addressing gender segregation in educational choices and increasing women’s participation in STEM, with about 70 % of these jobs taken or created by women.

Improving gender equality would contribute to an increase in GDP per capita of up to 9.6% in 2050 in the EU.

Women entrepreneurs represent 36.2 percent of businesses in the U.S. and are employing more than 13 million people and generating $1.9 trillion in sales.

Women’s micro-enterprises in developing countries are lifting themselves and their families and helping people surrounding them to get out of poverty.

Women are 1.17 more likely than men to create social ventures rather than merely economic ventures, and 1.23 times more likely to pursue environmental ventures, than economically focused ventures, according to Global Women´s Entrepreneurship Research.
